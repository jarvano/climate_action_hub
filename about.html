Climate Science Fundamentals

üî¨ The Greenhouse Effect

Greenhouse gases trap heat in Earth's atmosphere, maintaining temperatures suitable for life. However, human activities have increased greenhouse gas concentrations, enhancing this effect and causing global warming.

üß™ Key Greenhouse Gases

  • CO2 (Carbon Dioxide): 76% of emissions, lasts thousands of years
  • CH4 (Methane): 16% of emissions, 84x more potent than CO2
  • N2O (Nitrous Oxide): 6% of emissions, 264x more potent than CO2
  • Fluorinated Gases: 2% of emissions, thousands of times more potent

üìà Climate Feedbacks

Climate feedbacks amplify warming: melting ice reduces reflectivity, thawing permafrost releases methane, and warming oceans absorb less CO2. These feedbacks make early action critical.

Renewable Energy Solutions

üåû Solar Power

Solar energy capacity has grown by 22% annually since 2010, becoming the cheapest electricity source in history. One hour of sunlight provides more energy than humanity uses in a year.

üí® Wind Energy

Wind power is now cost-competitive with fossil fuels in many regions. Modern wind turbines can power thousands of homes, with offshore wind offering enormous potential.

üåä Other Renewables

  • Hydroelectric: 16% of global electricity, reliable and established
  • Geothermal: Consistent baseload power from Earth's heat
  • Biomass: Carbon-neutral when sustainably managed
  • Marine Energy: Wave and tidal power with huge potential

üîã Energy Storage

Battery costs have fallen 89% since 2010, making renewable energy storage increasingly viable. Grid-scale storage enables 24/7 clean energy availability.

Industry & Transportation

üè≠ Industrial Emissions

Industry accounts for 21% of global CO2 emissions. Key solutions include:

  • Electrification of industrial processes
  • Green hydrogen for steel and cement production
  • Circular economy and material efficiency
  • Carbon capture and utilization (CCU)

üöó Transportation Transformation

Transportation produces 21% of global emissions. Solutions include:

  • Electric Vehicles: 6x more efficient than gasoline cars
  • Public Transit: Reduces per-capita emissions by 55%
  • Active Transport: Walking and cycling for short trips
  • Aviation Innovation: Sustainable aviation fuels and efficiency

üèóÔ∏è Building Efficiency

Buildings use 40% of global energy. Retrofitting for efficiency can reduce energy use by 50-90% while improving comfort and health.

Nature-Based Solutions

üå≥ Forest Conservation

Forests absorb 2.6 Gt CO2 annually (30% of emissions). Protecting existing forests is more effective than planting new ones, as mature forests store vast carbon reserves.

üåä Ocean Solutions

  • Mangrove Restoration: 4x more carbon-dense than rainforests
  • Seagrass Protection: Stores 10% of ocean carbon in 0.2% of area
  • Kelp Farming: Fast-growing carbon capture and food source
  • Coral Protection: Maintains ocean carbon cycling

üåæ Regenerative Agriculture

Soil can store 3x more carbon than the atmosphere. Regenerative practices include cover crops, reduced tillage, and compost application, potentially sequestering 23 Gt CO2 by 2050.

üåø Urban Greening

Urban trees provide cooling, air purification, and carbon storage. Green roofs and walls reduce building energy use while providing habitat and improving air quality.
