Climate Science Basics

üå°Ô∏è What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While climate variations are natural, scientific evidence shows that human activities have been the dominant driver since the mid-20th century.

üí® Understanding CO2

Carbon dioxide (CO2) is the primary greenhouse gas emitted through human activities. It accounts for about 76% of total greenhouse gas emissions and can remain in the atmosphere for thousands of years.

üéØ The 1.5¬∞C Target

The Paris Agreement aims to limit global warming to well below 2¬∞C above pre-industrial levels, with efforts to limit it to 1.5¬∞C. This requires reducing global emissions by 45% by 2030.

‚ö° Renewable Energy

Transitioning to renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power is crucial for reducing emissions. These technologies are becoming increasingly cost-competitive with fossil fuels.
